# Structured data in 2026: the schema types LLMs are paying attention to

Schema.org markup has been a search engine best practice for over a decade. What has become
clear in the past two years is that structured data also plays a significant role in how LLMs
understand, index, and cite web content.

## Why LLMs respond to structured data

Large language models are trained on web content. Schema markup provides machine-readable
signals about what a page is, who produced it, and what it contains. During inference — when
an LLM is deciding which sources to cite or summarise — these signals influence authority.

## Schema types with the highest GEO impact in 2026

### Organization and Person

Establishing a clear, consistent entity representation for your brand and key people is
foundational. `Organization` schema with `sameAs` references to authoritative profiles
(LinkedIn, Wikidata, Companies House) builds entity confidence across the web.

### Article and BlogPosting

Proper authorship markup (with `Person` references), publication dates, and `about` tags
allow LLMs to attribute content correctly and assess recency.

### FAQPage

Directly answering common questions in FAQ schema creates content that is inherently
structured for extraction. This format maps naturally to how LLMs construct responses.

### HowTo

Step-by-step instructional content with `HowTo` schema is disproportionately cited by AI
systems when answering process or implementation questions.

## What to avoid

Overloading pages with irrelevant schema types creates noise. Focus on accuracy and relevance
over coverage. A small number of precisely marked-up pages outperforms a large number of
poorly marked-up ones consistently.
