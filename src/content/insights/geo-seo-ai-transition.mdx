# Why your SEO strategy won't survive the AI search transition

The rules of search are being rewritten in real time. Google's AI Overviews now answer millions
of queries before a user ever sees a list of blue links. Perplexity synthesises answers from
multiple sources and cites them inline. ChatGPT browses the web and returns direct responses.

For organisations that have invested years in traditional SEO, this is a fundamental disruption
— not a trend to monitor, but a shift to respond to now.

## What changes when AI answers first

Traditional SEO optimises for ranking — getting your page to the top of a results list.
GEO optimises for citation — getting your content surfaced as the answer, or as a trusted source
within an AI-generated response.

These require different signals. LLMs favour:

- **Semantic clarity** — content that is unambiguous and directly answers specific questions
- **Structured authority** — schema markup, clear authorship, and topical depth
- **Consistent entity signals** — your brand, people, and products described consistently across the web

## What you can do now

1. Audit your content for question-answer clarity
2. Implement structured data (Schema.org) on all key pages
3. Build topical clusters that demonstrate depth on your core subjects
4. Monitor where and how AI systems cite you — and where competitors are cited instead

GEO is not a replacement for good content. It is a framework for making sure that good content
is legible to the systems that are increasingly mediating how people find it.
